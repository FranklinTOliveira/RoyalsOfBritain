Upon the death of Queen Anne, the Crown of Great Britain went to George, Prince of Hanover.

Before George, the sons and daughters of the King were Lords and Ladies. The Hanoverians introduced the ranks of Prince and Princess for these royalty. The Hanoverian monarchs were Kings George I-IV, followed by Queen Victoria.

Under the Hanoverians, Parliament acquired the power to elect the King's ministers. In a similar spirit of democracy, the Kingdom of Ireland was released from British dependency in 1783. However, when its British-aligned elite were faced with Republican insurrections, the elites in both Dublin and London established the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland in 1800.
